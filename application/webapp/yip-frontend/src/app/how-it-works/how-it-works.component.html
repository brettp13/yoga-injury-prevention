HOW IT WORKS

YIP.GURU SIMPLIFIES SAFETY IN YOGA PRACTICE

The twin goals of Yoga Injury Prevention (YIP) are to help people with physical or medical conditions to maintain a safe and fulfilling yoga practice, and to utilize the beneficial aspects of yoga to improve, and to even heal those conditions.

We approach this in different ways:

POSTURES

Before beginning a pose, it’s helpful to review which conditions make it risky. If you or your student is practicing a pose, find out what it helps and what it hurts—with some videos.

MEDICAL CONDITIONS

If somebody has one or more conditions, see which poses are compatible with them all, which poses will benefit one or more of them, and which should not be done—and why.

WORKAROUNDS

Most contraindications are relative. Each contraindication is given a workaround. For workarounds contraindicated by another condition, simply view the poses suited for all conditions, and stick with them.

UNSAFE POSES

Injuries occur doing poses that have no medical contraindication. Proper alignment and judicious use of props protect you. Detailed instructions, videos, and blogs are here to help.

BENEFICIAL POSES

Author experience and the scientific yoga literature combine to direct you to the most effective postures for 100 conditions, or, advise where yoga has no proven benefit.

VIDEOS & BLOG

Physicians illustrate how to use yoga for benefit for e.g., rotator cuff, knee injury and insomnia. Blogs by leaders such as Matthew Taylor, J. Brown, Julie Carmen, Amy Weintraub.
